BudgetVault v3.0 is a complete UX revolution rebrand, not incremental features.

**Why:** Every budgeting app is spreadsheet UX with rounded corners. v3.0 makes BudgetVault feel like a game, not a spreadsheet.

**How to apply:** This is the next major initiative after v2.0 ships. All four pillars should ship together as one big rebrand.

## Four Pillars

### 1. Daily Allowance as THE Product (Spending Dial)
- Full-screen circular dial showing today's allowance is the default view
- Dial fills as you spend (like inverted Apple Watch ring)
- Green/yellow/red as you approach limit
- Tap center to log expense
- Swipe up for details, down to dismiss
- This is 90% of app usage

### 2. Three-Mode Swipe Navigation (Kill Tab Bar)
- **Spend Mode** (center/default) -- The daily dial. "How much can I spend right now?"
- **Review Mode** (swipe left) -- The timeline. "Where did my money go?"
- **Plan Mode** (swipe right) -- The envelopes. "How should I allocate?"
- Three swipes, three mental modes, no tabs, no navigation hierarchy

### 3. Physical Envelope Metaphor (Wallet View)
- Swipe through envelope cards like Apple Wallet
- Each shows cash "inside" that visually shrinks
- Drag money between envelopes with finger (replaces MoveMoneyView)
- Tap to "open" -- transactions spill out
- Empty envelope: crinkle haptic. Overdrawn: red burn particle effect

### 4. Conversational Onboarding (Chat Interface)
- Chat-style: "What's your monthly take-home?" -> user types/speaks
- Builds categories conversationally, not via forms
- Can revisit anytime: "Hey BudgetVault, I got a raise"
- Feels like texting a financial advisor

## Supporting Concepts

### Gesture-First Entry
- Pull down on dial for quick entry
- Voice: "twelve fifty at grocery store" -> parsed, categorized, done
- Shake to undo last transaction
- Long press dial for yesterday vs today
- Swipe between days like calendar
- Haptic detents as budget tightens

### Spending Timeline
- Horizontal scrollable timeline (like iOS Photos years view)
- Each day = dot (size = amount, color = over/under)
- Pinch to zoom: days -> weeks -> months -> year
- Patterns emerge visually: "I always overspend on Fridays"

### Emotional Design
- Good day: warm colors, encouragement
- Tough day: no shame, "Tomorrow's a fresh start"
- Streak milestones: Duolingo-style full-screen celebrations
- Month end under budget: vault door opening, coins pouring
- Month end over: gentle, constructive, private

### Ambient Awareness
- Live Activity on lock screen with daily allowance
- Dynamic Island pulse approaching daily limit
- Watch complication with remaining daily amount
- StandBy mode showing spending dial
- Smart morning notification with actual daily budget + category status
