## Posting Strategy Notes

### Timing Rationale
| Time Slot | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| 7:30-8:15 AM EST | Catches morning scroll before work. Best for educational content and tips. |
| 12:00-12:30 PM EST | Lunch break browsing. Best for provocative/engagement content. |
| 6:00 PM EST | Evening wind-down. Best for storytelling and behind-the-scenes. |
| 10:00-11:00 AM EST (weekends) | Weekend browsing peaks later. Best for feature demos and reflection. |

### Engagement Rules
1. **Reply to every comment in the first 2 hours.** This is non-negotiable for algorithm visibility.
2. **Never be defensive.** If a YNAB fan defends their app, say "YNAB is great software. We just built something for people who want a different model."
3. **Quote-tweet positive replies** with a brief thank you. This amplifies social proof.
4. **Never delete negative replies** unless they are abusive. Disagreement drives reach.
5. **Follow back indie devs and personal finance creators** who engage. Build the network.

### Hashtag Strategy
- Use 2-3 hashtags maximum per tweet. More looks spammy on X.
- Rotate between niche (#envelopebudgeting, #ondeviceAI) and broad (#personalfinance, #budgeting).
- Always include #budgetvault on feature and launch tweets to build brand searchability.
- Monitor #ynab and #budgetapp for conversation opportunities (reply helpfully, never spam).

### Content to Prepare in Advance
- [ ] 3-4 clean App Store screenshots (dashboard, insights, envelope view, privacy label)
- [ ] 15-second screen recording of onboarding flow (no account, instant setup)
- [ ] 15-second screen recording of ML insights screen
- [ ] App Store privacy nutrition label screenshot
- [ ] Profile banner updated with launch messaging
- [ ] Bio updated: "Privacy-first envelope budgeting. $9.99 once, not $100/year. No accounts, no servers, no data collection. budgetvault.io"

### Platform Cross-Posting Adaptation
These tweets are written for X/Twitter. To adapt for other platforms:

**Reddit (r/ynab, r/privacy, r/iphone, r/personalfinance):**
- Rewrite Day 2 and Day 12 as text posts titled "Why I built a one-time-purchase budgeting app"
- Lead with the story, not the pitch. Reddit downvotes anything that smells like an ad.
- Disclose that you are the developer in the first line.

**Product Hunt:**
- Launch on a Tuesday or Wednesday for best visibility.
- Tagline: "Envelope budgeting that never leaves your device"
- Use Day 8 content as the foundation for the PH description.

**Hacker News (Show HN):**
- Title: "Show HN: BudgetVault -- On-device envelope budgeting with local ML insights"
- Lead with the technical decisions (on-device ML, no backend, SwiftData architecture).
- HN respects technical depth over marketing polish.

3. **Budgeting myth-busting** -- "You don't need to track every latte. Here's what actually matters."
4. **Privacy news commentary** -- React to data breach headlines with "Meanwhile, BudgetVault stores nothing"
5. **Milestone celebrations** -- Downloads, ratings, reviews (keep authentic, not braggy)
6. **Seasonal content** -- Tax season budgeting, holiday spending, back-to-school budgets
7. **Envelope method tutorials** -- Short educational threads that organically mention BudgetVault
8. **Competitor pricing changes** -- When subscription apps raise prices, post about one-time pricing
