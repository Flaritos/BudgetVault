## 5. Profile Settings

### Account Type
- Switch to **Professional Account** (free)
- Category: **App**
- This unlocks Twitter Analytics, promoted tweets (if needed later), and the professional profile format

### Direct Messages
- **Open DMs to everyone** -- You want users to be able to report bugs and ask questions. Early adopters who DM you become your best advocates.
- Turn ON "Allow message requests from everyone"
- Turn OFF "Filter low-quality messages" (you do not have enough volume for this to matter yet; you do not want to miss real messages)

### Notifications
- Turn ON notifications for **mentions and replies** -- You must reply to every comment in the first 2 hours. This is non-negotiable for algorithm visibility.
- Turn ON notifications for **new followers** during launch week (so you can follow back indie devs and finance creators who engage)
- Turn OFF notifications for likes (noise)

### Content Settings
- Turn OFF "Sensitive content" warning on your tweets
- Turn ON "Protect your tweets" = NO (public account)
- Turn ON "Photo tagging" = Anyone can tag

### Profile Extras
- Add the App Store link as a **profile spotlight** (Professional accounts can pin a link)
- Set up **Automated DM welcome message** (optional, only if it does not feel spammy):
  ```
  Thanks for following! BudgetVault is a privacy-first envelope budgeting app. Free to try, $9.99 to unlock everything. No account needed. budgetvault.io
  ```

### Twitter Blue / Premium
- Do NOT subscribe at launch. The blue checkmark on a brand-new account with low followers looks inauthentic.
- Revisit at 500+ followers. At that point, the longer tweet limit (4,000 chars) and edit button become useful.

## 7. Account Warm-Up Strategy

A brand-new account posting promotional content gets flagged by the algorithm. Spend 2-3 days before Day 1 doing this:

1. Follow accounts from your lists (30-50, not all at once)
2. Like 10-15 posts per day from personal finance and indie dev accounts
3. Reply thoughtfully to 5-10 posts per day (not "great post!" -- add genuine insight)
4. Retweet 2-3 relevant posts about budgeting, privacy, or indie development
5. Do NOT post any original content yet

This establishes the account as a real participant, not a broadcast bot. After 2-3 days of warm-up, post the Day 1 pinned tweet.
