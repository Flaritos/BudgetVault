# Revenue: Investment Researcher Findings

## TL;DR
At $18K/yr ARR with a $14.99 one-time IAP, BudgetVault sits on a 4-6x revenue gap to comparable indie iOS productivity apps (Things, Soulver, Bear); the binding constraint is not pricing power, it is install volume, and the next $100K of ARR comes from a paid-up-front "Family Pack" SKU plus localization, not from another feature pile.

## Top 3 Opportunities (Ranked)
1. **$24.99 "Family Pack" non-consumable SKU on top of $14.99 base (post-CKShare)** — Apple permits multiple non-consumable IAPs on a free app, preserving the "no subscription" wedge. Things ships per-platform SKUs ($9.99 iPhone / $19.99 iPad / $49.99 Mac = $79.97 total; Cultured Code reportedly ~$192K-$1M revenue range with <15 employees per RocketReach/Owler). A $14.99 + $24.99 ladder ARPPU-blends to ~$18-20 vs. $14.99 today (+20-33% per conversion). Effort 3-4 wks (CKShare). Impact: +$30-50K/yr at current install volume.
2. **Localization to DE/ES/FR (Roadmap T4)** — Personal finance app TAM is ~$31-167B (range across Fact.MR, Grand View, Business Research Co.); EU represents ~25% of the iOS-paid productivity buyer base and over-indexes on privacy willingness-to-pay (GDPR-trained users). Soulver/Things/iA Writer all derive material % from non-English. Effort 3-4 wks one-time + ~$1.5K translation. Impact: +30-60% addressable installs, ~$5-10K/yr incremental at current conversion.
3. **Apple Search Ads spend reallocation to $24.99 SKU bidding** — At a 15% Apple cut (Small Business Program, you keep 85% of $14.99 = $12.74 net), payback math allows CPI up to ~$1.00 at 8% conversion. Current Tier 1 plan budgets $390-600/mo (marketing-plan.md). Reallocate 60% to "envelope budgeting" + "ynab alternative" exact-match where intent is highest. Effort: ongoing. Impact: doubling install volume from 50→100/day = $18K → $46K/yr (per ROADMAP base case).

## Top 3 Risks / Debt Items
1. **Single-platform concentration** — 100% of revenue on iOS App Store. Apple DMA/policy shifts (sideloading rules, US App Store regulation) are a binary tail risk. Mitigation: keep Small Business Program eligibility (cap below $1M proceeds is non-binding for years) and ensure CSV export is bulletproof so user migration story is "your data is yours" — defensive moat against forced refunds.
2. **AI commoditization of categorization** — On-device LLMs in iOS 19+ make "AI categorization" table stakes within 18 months. The privacy wedge holds (still no cloud), but feature parity erodes price defense. Thesis breaker: if a free Apple-native budgeting feature ships in iOS 19 or 20, repositioning to "envelope methodology + Live Activities + Watch" is required.
3. **Recession exposure asymmetry** — Counterintuitively, budgeting app demand is countercyclical (good), but $14.99 one-time is a discretionary purchase (bad). Comparable: Soulver/Bear saw ~10-20% conversion compression in 2022-2023 per indie dev forums. Build a $4.99 "starter" tier? No — it cannibalizes. Instead, hold price and accept volatility.

## Quick Wins (<1 day each)
- Ship the $14.99 → keep the price (already done); add countdown urgency only when raising to $24.99.
- Add "Gift this app" via App Store gift links in Settings — zero-effort referral channel, comparable to Things.
- Surface lifetime-value framing on paywall: "$14.99 once vs. YNAB $109/yr — break even in 50 days."
- Enroll/verify Small Business Program (15% cut) — confirms 85% net on every sale.

## Long Bets (>2 weeks but transformative)
- **Mac Catalyst SKU at $24.99-29.99** — unlocks the Soulver/Things-style multi-platform ladder without violating any constraint. Adds a third price point and qualifies as "lifestyle business → sellable asset" inflection.
- **Acquisition-readiness positioning** — Indie iOS finance apps are acquired by RevenueCat-adjacent rollups and EU privacy-tech holdcos at 3-5x ARR for profitable, retentive bases. At $200K ARR, BudgetVault is a $600K-$1M acquisition target; at $500K ARR with partner sharing, $1.5-2.5M. Document MRR/retention monthly to support diligence.

## What NOT to Do
- **Subscription tier** — destroys the wedge; ARR uplift not worth the brand cost (rejected by all 5 prior agents).
- **Lower price to $9.99** — Things iPhone is $9.99 *and* charges separately for iPad/Mac; a single-SKU race to the bottom yields lower ARPPU without conversion lift (price elasticity for paid productivity apps is ~-0.4, per indie dev surveys).
- **Bundle with a sister app** — premature; build BudgetVault to $200K ARR first.
- **Take outside funding** — at <$200K ARR with no growth team, dilution is unjustified; YNAB stayed bootstrapped (per Crunchbase) and is now ~$30M+ ARR.
